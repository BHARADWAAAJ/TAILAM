# Standard Implementation Note — IEC 60567

**Standard:** IEC 60567, *Oil-filled electrical equipment — Sampling of
gases and analysis of free and dissolved gases — Guidance.*
**Status in TAILAM:** Referenced, not computationally implemented.
**This document does not reproduce any text from the standard.**

## Purpose

IEC 60567 governs how an oil sample is drawn from a transformer and how
the laboratory extracts and measures dissolved gas concentrations from
that sample. It is the standard that makes the *inputs* TAILAM consumes
(gas concentrations in ppm) meaningful and comparable across laboratories.

## Scope of TAILAM's implementation

TAILAM does not implement any calculation from IEC 60567 — sampling
procedure and laboratory extraction method are outside the scope of a
DGA interpretation calculator; they happen before any value is typed into
TAILAM. IEC 60567 is listed in TAILAM's Engineering References section
because the diagnostic methods TAILAM does implement (Duval Triangles,
Rogers, IEC 60599 three-ratio, IEEE C57.104, Key Gas, Doernenburg, CIGRE)
all assume gas concentrations obtained per this standard's sampling and
analysis guidance. Citing it is a transparency measure — it tells the
engineer what assumption underlies every number they typed in — not a
claim that TAILAM performs any part of the sampling or lab process.

## How TAILAM implements it

No engine function corresponds to this standard. It appears only as a
citation in the Engineering References section of both the Main Tank and
OLTC Engineering Workspace.

## Limitations

- TAILAM has no way to verify that a given gas-concentration input was
  actually obtained per IEC 60567 procedure — this is a trust boundary at
  data entry, not something software can validate.
- TAILAM does not track sample provenance, sampling date vs. analysis
  date, or laboratory identity. See `docs/release/ROADMAP.md` for future
  laboratory-integration scope.
